Understanding Physical Activity

What is Physical Activity?

Physical activity refers to any bodily movement produced by skeletal muscles that results in energy expenditure.

It encompasses a wide range of activities, from everyday tasks like walking or climbing stairs to structured exercise routines and sports.

Benefits of Physical Activity

  • Cardiovascular Health: Strengthens the heart and improves circulation.
  • Weight Management: Helps maintain a healthy weight by burning calories.
  • Musculoskeletal Health: Strengthens muscles and bones, reducing the risk of osteoporosis.
  • Mental Well-being: Reduces symptoms of depression and anxiety.
  • Longevity: Associated with longer life expectancy and better quality of life.

Characteristics of Physical Activity

  • Intensity: Ranges from light (e.g., walking) to vigorous (e.g., sprinting).
  • Duration: Can be short or prolonged based on fitness goals.
  • Frequency: At least 150 minutes/week of moderate-intensity activity is recommended.

Functions of Physical Activity

  • Improves overall physical fitness (strength, endurance, flexibility).
  • Promotes health by reducing chronic disease risk.
  • Supports weight management.
  • Enhances mental health and cognitive function.
  • Encourages social interaction and community bonding.

Ways to Improve Physical Activity

  • Choose walking or cycling over driving.
  • Get off the bus/train a stop earlier and walk.
  • Walk children to school.
  • Exercise to boost endorphins and improve mood and sleep.

Where to Get Help

  • Your family doctor
  • Local gym or fitness center
  • Community centers
  • Physiotherapists

Conclusion

Regular physical activity is vital for health, helping prevent chronic diseases, boost mental well-being, and improve quality of life. Everyone can benefit from incorporating more movement into their daily routines.
